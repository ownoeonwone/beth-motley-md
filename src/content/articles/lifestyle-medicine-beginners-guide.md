Lifestyle medicine is a medical specialty that uses evidence-based therapeutic lifestyle interventions as a primary modality to prevent, treat, and often reverse chronic disease. It is not alternative medicine; it is what the evidence says works.

## The Six Pillars

The American College of Lifestyle Medicine identifies six interconnected pillars that form the foundation of health:

### 1. Whole-Food, Plant-Predominant Nutrition
A dietary pattern centered on vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds provides the nutrients our bodies need while reducing inflammation and supporting metabolic health.

### 2. Regular Physical Activity
Movement is medicine. Even moderate regular activity (150 minutes per week) dramatically reduces risk for heart disease, diabetes, cancer, and depression.

### 3. Restorative Sleep
Quality sleep of 7-9 hours per night is essential for immune function, metabolic regulation, cognitive performance, and emotional resilience.

### 4. Stress Management
Chronic stress drives inflammation and disease. Evidence-based approaches like mindfulness, breathing exercises, and time in nature help regulate the stress response.

### 5. Positive Social Connection
Strong social relationships are as important for longevity as not smoking. Community, belonging, and meaningful relationships are foundational to health.

### 6. Avoidance of Risky Substances
Minimizing or eliminating tobacco, excess alcohol, and other harmful substances removes major drivers of preventable disease.

## Why It Matters

Chronic diseases (heart disease, type 2 diabetes, obesity, certain cancers) account for the vast majority of healthcare spending and premature death. Yet up to 80% of these conditions are preventable or reversible through lifestyle changes.

Lifestyle medicine is not about perfection. It is about making sustainable, evidence-based changes that compound over time. Small steps, consistently applied, lead to remarkable results.
